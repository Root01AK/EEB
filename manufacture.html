Lathe Machine:

Turning: This involves rotating a workpiece against a cutting tool to remove material and create cylindrical shapes, such as shafts, pins, and bushings.
Facing: This operation is used to create a flat surface perpendicular to the axis of rotation of the workpiece.
Drilling: Manual lathes can be equipped with drill bits to create holes in the workpiece.
Boring: This operation enlarges existing holes or creates internal features, such as bores or recesses.
Threading: Manual lathes can be used to cut external or internal threads on cylindrical workpieces.
Knurling: This process creates a textured pattern on the surface of the workpiece for improved grip or aesthetics.
Parting: Parting or grooving operations are used to cut off a section of material from the workpiece, creating separate parts.
Taper Turning: Manual lathes can be used to create tapered features, such as conical shapes or angled surfaces.

Pillar Drilling Machine:

Drilling: Creating holes in various materials such as metal, wood, and plastic.
Reaming: Enlarging or finishing existing holes to precise dimensions.
Counterboring: Creating recessed holes to accommodate bolt heads or nuts.
Tapping: Cutting internal threads into drilled holes for fasteners.
Countersinking: Creating conical recesses to allow flathead screws to sit flush with the surface.

Bandsaw Machine:

Straight Cutting: Cutting straight lines through various materials such as metal, wood, and plastic.

Automatic Tube Cutting Machine:

Precision Cutting: Cutting tubes and pipes to specific lengths with high accuracy.
High-Speed Cutting: Rapidly cutting multiple tubes in production environments.
Burr-Free Cutting: Producing clean and smooth cuts without burrs or sharp edges.
Angle Cutting: Cutting tubes at various angles for specific applications.
Bundle Cutting: Simultaneously cutting multiple tubes within a bundle for efficiency in batch processing.
